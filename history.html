Key Milestones in Version Control History

  1. 1972 – SCCS: First VCS, used on IBM mainframes at Bell Labs.
  2. 1982 – RCS: Automated tracking for single files.
  3. 1986 – CVS: Enabled team collaboration with client-server model.
  4. 2000 – Subversion (SVN): Added atomic commits and improved branching.
  5. 2005 – Git & Mercurial: Distributed version control revolution.
  6. 2008 – GitHub: Made sharing and collaboration easy online.
